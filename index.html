About

Caring for the critically ill,
building tools to do it better.

Dr. Soumya Murag is an Assistant Professor in Pulmonary and Critical Care Medicine at the University of California–San Francisco, where she serves as Director of the Pulmonary Function Testing Laboratory at the Mt. Zion campus.

She received her B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Southern California and her M.D. from Virginia Commonwealth University School of Medicine, and completed her Internal Medicine residency and Chief Residency at Santa Clara Valley Medical Center before pursuing her Pulmonary and Critical Care Medicine Fellowship at UC Irvine.

Clinically, she attends in the Medical ICU, the outpatient Pulmonary clinic across multiple UCSF sites, and contributes to the inpatient pulmonary consult service. She has served in advisory and clinical validation roles for digital health startups at the intersection of respiratory medicine and artificial intelligence.

Her scholarly interests span AI-enabled pulmonary diagnostics, remote monitoring for respiratory conditions, precision medicine for obstructive lung disease, and point-of-care ultrasound education. She is a passionate advocate for the integration of digital health tools into pulmonary and critical care practice and speaks and writes on the evolving role of artificial intelligence in clinical medicine and physician identity.
